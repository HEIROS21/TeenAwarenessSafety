Why This Matters!!

Sexual harassment is a serious issue that affects teenagers worldwide. Whether in schools, online, or public spaces, many teens experience unwanted attention, comments, or actions that make them feel uncomfortable or unsafe. Raising awareness is the first step to creating a safer environment for everyone. By understanding what sexual harassment is, how to recognize it, and how to take action, we can break the silence and stand against harassment together.

What is Sexual Harassment?

Sexual harassment is any unwelcome behavior of a sexual nature that makes a person feel uncomfortable, threatened, or disrespected. It can happen in different forms:

Verbal: Inappropriate comments, sexual jokes, or catcalling.

Physical: Unwanted touching, groping, or blocking someone’s path.

Visual: Sending or showing inappropriate images or gestures.

Cyber: Harassment through text messages, social media, or online platforms.

How to Recognize Sexual Harassment

Sometimes, it can be unclear if a behavior is inappropriate. Use these questions to help identify harassment

Would it make others feel unsafe?

Does it make you feel uncomfortable, scared, or pressured?

Is it unwanted and persistent?

Would the person stop if asked?

If you answer "yes" to any of these, it may be harassment. Trust your feelings and seek help.

What to Do If You Experience or Witness Sexual Harassment

If you are a victim or witness harassment, take these steps:

Speak Up

If safe, tell the harasser their behavior is not acceptable.

Document It

Keep records of texts, messages, or incidents.

Talk to Someone

Confide in a trusted adult, friend, or counselor.

Report It

 If it happens at school or online, report it to the appropriate authority.

Seek Support

ou are not alone. Reach out to organizations that can help.

The Power of Consent

Consent is the foundation of respectful interactions. It means freely giving permission without pressure or fear.

01

02

No one should feel forced or uncomfortable.

03
